Website Evaluations

Apple.com

Apple's website is a great example of clean, modern design. It's easy to navigate and looks very professional with beautiful product images and simple organization.

  • Contrast: Apple uses great contrast with large product images against white or black backgrounds. The headings are much bigger than body text, and the blue buttons clearly stand out.
  • Repetition: The site uses the same fonts, colors, and layouts on every page. All product cards look identical with consistent spacing and button styles, making everything feel unified.
  • Alignment: Everything lines up perfectly in organized columns and rows. Nothing looks randomly placed, which makes the site look clean and professional.
  • Proximity: Related information is grouped together nicely. Prices are near buy buttons, and there's good white space between sections so you can tell what goes together.

Yale School of Art

The Yale School of Art website is one of the worst-designed sites on the internet. Despite being from a famous art school, it's messy, hard to use, and breaks most basic design rules.

  • Contrast: The site has terrible contrast with random background colors that make text hard to read. There's no clear hierarchy, so you can't tell what's important.
  • Repetition: There's no consistency at all. Every page uses different colors, fonts, and layouts. The navigation changes on each page, making it feel like random pages stuck together.
  • Alignment: Elements are scattered everywhere with no clear structure. Text and images are placed randomly, making it look messy and unprofessional.
  • Proximity: Related information is spread far apart while unrelated things are too close together. There's barely any white space, making the layout cramped and confusing.
